# Mockup Brief — 5 Etsy Listing Images

**Phase:** 4 — Canva Design (final step before Phase 5)
**Why this matters:** Mockups are the #1 lever on Etsy click-through rate. Premium-feeling mockups will out-convert competitor listings even if your underlying product were identical (and ours isn't — ours is better).
**Tool options:** Canva Smart Mockups (built into Canva Pro) OR Placeit.net OR a clean Canva composition with stock backgrounds.

---

## Etsy upload spec

- Listing accepts up to 10 images. We deliver 5 (high quality > volume).
- Recommended size: **2000 × 2000 px** (square) — Etsy displays them as squares in search.
- Format: **JPG**, sRGB color profile, file size under 1 MB each.
- File naming:
  ```
  30-day-life-reset/exports/mockups/
    ├── 01_cover_flatlay.jpg
    ├── 02_interior_spread.jpg
    ├── 03_habit_tracker_closeup.jpg
    ├── 04_phone_screen.jpg
    └── 05_lifestyle_bundle.jpg
  ```

---

## Mockup 1 — Cover Flat Lay

**Purpose:** The hero image. This is what shoppers see in search. It must read well at thumbnail size.

**Composition:**
- Top-down (true flat lay) angle.
- Surface: neutral linen, oat-colored. Slight visible weave texture. Avoid wood — wood reads "rustic" and our brand is "wellness."
- Workbook cover laid flat on the linen, slightly off-center (rule of thirds — cover sits in the right two-thirds, props in the left).
- Props (keep minimal):
  - One ceramic mug of tea, top-down view, steam optional
  - One small sage or eucalyptus sprig (echoes the cover botanical)
  - A small pair of glasses or a fine-tip pen
  - **Three props maximum.** More feels cluttered.
- Lighting: soft natural daylight from one side. No harsh shadows. No direct flash.
- Color cast: warm/neutral. Avoid blue or cool tones.

**Tool path:**
- Canva Smart Mockups: select "Book on linen" mockup → upload front cover image → adjust lighting and shadow → download as 2000×2000.
- OR Placeit.net: search "magazine flat lay linen" → upload cover → adjust → download.

**Thumbnail readability test:** Shrink to 200×200 px and check that the title is still readable. If not, the cover image itself needs a stronger title hierarchy.

## Mockup 2 — Interior Spread (Days 5–6 Open)

**Purpose:** Shows the buyer what they're getting inside. Crucial for conversion — most shoppers won't buy a workbook without seeing inside.

**Composition:**
- Open book mockup, two-page spread visible.
- Left page: Day 5 of your workbook. Right page: Day 6.
- Use a clean spiral-bound or paperback book mockup — even though we sell digital, the *feel* of a printed book signals quality.
- Same linen background as Mockup 1 (consistency across the listing image set).
- Props: optional single sage sprig in the corner.

**How to make it:**
1. Export Days 5 and 6 as JPGs from Canva (right-click page → Download as JPG).
2. Find a book mockup template — Placeit "Open book mockup" works well; Canva Smart Mockups has "Open notebook on table."
3. Upload your two pages into the mockup's left/right slots.
4. Render at 2000×2000.

**QA:** Confirm the daily page text is still legible at 2000×2000. If it's blurry, re-export the source pages at higher DPI.

## Mockup 3 — Habit Tracker Close-Up

**Purpose:** Shows the practical, hands-on nature of the workbook. Trackers are a high-search keyword and worth their own image.

**Composition:**
- Close-up angle on the Monthly Habit Tracker grid (page 45).
- Slight 3/4 angle (not flat top-down) — gives depth and feels more "in use."
- A pen partially placed across the page, suggesting the tracker is being filled in.
- A few cells already filled in (sage open circles → filled circles) — implies someone is actively using it.

**How to make it:**
1. Export page 45 as a JPG.
2. Use Canva or Photoshop to manually fill in 4–6 of the tracker circles (you can do this in Canva by drawing small sage circles on top).
3. Place into a Smart Mockup that shows a journal at an angle, OR composite onto a clean linen background with a pen overlay.

## Mockup 4 — Phone Screen Mockup

**Purpose:** Signals "use this on your phone too" — important because many buyers will read it on iPad or iPhone, not print.

**Composition:**
- iPhone or iPad mockup (Canva has both).
- On the screen: a daily page (use Day 1 — the strongest first impression).
- Background: same warm linen surface OR a soft gradient cream `#FAF8F6` field.
- Optional: a hand holding the phone, partial frame, soft focus.
- Add a subtle text overlay in one corner: "Open on any device" — small, sage, all-caps tracking.

**How to make it:**
1. Export Day 1 page as JPG.
2. Canva → search "iPhone mockup" → choose a clean front-facing iPhone screen template → drop in your Day 1 image.
3. Place the mockup on a `#FAF8F6` background. Add the small "Open on any device" caption in Inter 11 pt, sage `#848B6F`.
4. Render at 2000×2000.

## Mockup 5 — Lifestyle Bundle Shot

**Purpose:** The "wow" image. Combines all formats and props into one aspirational frame. Goes at slot 5 — last impression before they decide to buy.

**Composition:**
- Top-down flat lay, wider angle.
- Includes:
  - Cover (printed-book mockup or flat printout)
  - Phone showing a daily page (mini iPhone mockup composited in)
  - Open book showing interior spread (mini composition)
  - Linen surface base, sage sprig, mug, pen, small notebook
- Arrange in a pleasing diagonal — the eye should travel cover → phone → spread.
- More props OK here (this is the "bundle" shot) but stay tonal: cream, sage, oat. No bright colors.

**How to make it:**
- This is the highest-effort mockup. Block out 30+ minutes.
- Easiest route: in Canva, create a 2000×2000 design with a linen background, then drop in:
  - Mockup 1's output (resize to ~50%)
  - Mockup 2's output (resize to ~40%)
  - Mockup 4's output (resize to ~30%)
- Arrange them in a diagonal flow with overlapping shadows for depth.
- Add 2–3 sage leaf graphics around the edges.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why it hurts | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Wood backgrounds | Reads "farmhouse," wrong vibe | Use linen, marble, or cream paper |
| Overcrowded props | Looks busy, lowers perceived premium | Max 3 props in close-ups, max 6 in bundle shot |
| Cool blue lighting | Reads "office" or "corporate" | Warm daylight only |
| Bright color accents | Breaks the brand palette | Stay in cream/sage/oat range |
| Tiny title in cover mockup | Fails the thumbnail test | Test at 200×200 — title must be readable |
| Inconsistent surfaces across mockups | Looks like 5 different products | Use the same linen background as the through-line |
| Stock photo "happy woman with laptop" | Generic, off-brand | We sell calm, not hustle. Skip lifestyle photos with people for v1 |
