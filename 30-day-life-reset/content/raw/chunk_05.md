## WEEK 3 — GROWTH: Body, Mind, Environment

You've been building a foundation. This week goes a little deeper.

Week 3 is where you start to feel the difference — not dramatically, but in small ways. A little more ease. A little less friction. A quieter mind at the end of the day.

This week adds one new habit category: movement. Short, simple, and not about fitness goals. Just getting your body involved in the reset. Everything else builds on what you've already started.

### Day 15: Coming Back Again
**Goal:** Return to the work without making a story out of the gap — or the time away.
**Task 1:** Don't scroll back to calculate what you missed. Open to today, start here, and go forward from now.
**Task 2:** Write one sentence about where you are right now — energy, mood, headspace. Just the honest current state.
**Task 3:** Do your morning habit from Week 2, or whatever adjusted version you're using. One familiar action to re-anchor.
**Quick Win:** Make yourself something warm to drink. Sit with it for 2 minutes. You're back.
**Why It Works:** Re-entry without self-criticism is a learnable skill. The brain returns more easily when the cost of coming back is low.
**Reflection:** What made Week 3 harder to start than Week 1 or 2? What got in the way?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow you'll add movement — 10 minutes, nothing intense. Just your body, briefly awake.

> Coming back after a gap takes more courage than starting. You did it. That counts for more than a perfect streak.

### Day 16: Move for 10 Minutes
**Goal:** Add one short daily movement — not for fitness, just to feel more present in your body.
**Task 1:** Walk outside for 10 minutes. No podcast, no phone. Just walk slowly and look at what's around you.
**Task 2:** When you're done, write one word for how your body felt before the walk and one word for after.
**Task 3:** Choose a specific time tomorrow you'll repeat this. Write it down: "Tomorrow at [time], I'll walk for 10 minutes."
**Quick Win:** Stand up and stretch for 60 seconds right now. Any stretch. Just move something.
**Why It Works:** Brief movement raises mood-regulating neurotransmitters and reduces physical tension faster than almost any other habit.
**Reflection:** When was the last time you moved your body without it being for a specific outcome or goal?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow is the halfway point. You'll pause and take stock of how far you've actually come.

### Day 18: Clear Your Head
**Goal:** Reduce one source of mental clutter that's been running in the background without you choosing it.
**Task 1:** Brain dump for 5 minutes — everything in your head right now. Worries, tasks, loops, random thoughts. All of it out.
**Task 2:** Read what you wrote. Circle one item that's actually within your control right now. Just one.
**Task 3:** Take one small action on it — a reply, a note, a decision, one concrete step. Keep it under 2 minutes.
**Quick Win:** Close 5 browser tabs or app windows you haven't touched in days. Small clears add up quickly.
**Why It Works:** Writing down mental load frees working memory and makes problems feel more contained and manageable.
**Reflection:** What thought has been on repeat for you lately that you haven't actually addressed or resolved?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow is about your physical environment — a deeper clear than what you did in Week 1.

### Day 19: Your Environment, Continued
**Goal:** Improve one more corner of your physical space — building on the clear you did in Week 1.
**Task 1:** Choose a different space from Day 4 — a drawer, shelf, car, bag, or closet section. Set a 7-minute timer and start.
**Task 2:** Clear it out. Keep only what belongs, works, or you actually use. Bin, donate, or relocate everything else.
**Task 3:** Take a photo of the cleared space. Look at it when you feel overwhelmed this week. Proof that you create order.
**Quick Win:** Empty one thing right now — a bag, a pocket, a small tray. 60 seconds flat.
**Why It Works:** Physical order measurably lowers cortisol — your nervous system responds directly to what your eyes take in.
**Reflection:** Which area of your life feels most cluttered right now — physical space, your mind, or your relationships?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow you'll take one step toward something you've been quietly putting off.

### Day 20: The Thing You've Been Avoiding
**Goal:** Get curious about one thing you've been circling around — without any pressure to start it today.
**Task 1:** Write down the thing you've been avoiding. Don't plan it or judge it — just name it clearly on paper.
**Task 2:** Write one honest sentence about why this thing has been waiting. What have you been telling yourself about it?
**Task 3:** Write what it would feel like to have simply started it — not finished it, just started. Notice what comes up.
**Quick Win:** Write: "The thing I've been circling is ___." Naming it clearly is itself a small act of honesty.
**Why It Works:** Understanding why we avoid something is often more useful than forcing a start before we're ready.
**Reflection:** What's one small thing that would need to be true before starting this felt manageable to you?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow wraps Week 3 — and starts asking bigger questions about what you actually want.
