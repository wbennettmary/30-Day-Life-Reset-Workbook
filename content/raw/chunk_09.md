## BONUS 1 — 50 TINY HABITS

**Every habit on this list takes 5 minutes or less.**

These aren't goals. They're small, repeatable actions you can drop into any day — good or hard. Use them as inspiration, as replacements when your main habit breaks down, or just as a menu to pick from when you're not sure what to do next.

---

### BODY & HEALTH (9 habits)

1. Drink a full glass of water before you do anything else in the morning.
2. Take 5 slow, deliberate breaths. Exhale longer than you inhale.
3. Stretch for 2 minutes when you get out of bed — any movement counts.
4. Go to bed 15 minutes earlier than usual tonight.
5. Eat one piece of fruit or one vegetable you wouldn't normally bother with.
6. Walk around the block once. One loop, no destination required.
7. Close your eyes for 60 seconds in the middle of the day. Not sleeping — just stopping.
8. Open a window and get 2 minutes of fresh air.
9. Take any supplements or medications you've been skipping. Right now.

---

### MIND & MOOD (9 habits)

10. Write 3 things that went okay today. Not great — just okay.
11. Read one page of a book you've been meaning to start.
12. Sit quietly for 2 minutes without a screen. Just sit.
13. Write down one worry on paper, then close the notebook.
14. Choose one word for how you want to feel today. Write it somewhere visible.
15. Turn off all notifications for the next hour.
16. Spend 5 minutes outside — not exercising, just being outside.
17. Write one thing you're looking forward to, even if it's small.
18. Notice one beautiful or interesting thing in your immediate environment. Write it down.

---

### FOCUS & PRODUCTIVITY (8 habits)

19. Write your top 3 tasks for today on paper. Cross them off as you go.
20. Clear your phone home screen of everything you don't actually need there.
21. Set a 25-minute timer and work on one thing only until it goes off.
22. Decline or defer one thing this week that isn't genuinely urgent.
23. Make one small decision you've been putting off for more than a week.
24. Prepare your bag, clothes, or desk the night before to make tomorrow easier.
25. Spend 5 minutes at end of day writing what you'll do first tomorrow.
26. Check your email only at two set times today — not continuously.

---

### HOME & ENVIRONMENT (8 habits)

27. Clear one surface completely. Put back only what belongs.
28. Make your bed within 10 minutes of getting up.
29. Do the dishes immediately after eating — don't let them sit.
30. Put 5 objects back where they actually belong.
31. Take out the trash or recycling before it overflows.
32. Wipe down one kitchen or bathroom counter with a cloth.
33. Put away clean laundry within 24 hours of washing it.
34. Spend 3 minutes tidying the space where you spend most of your time.

---

### MONEY & FINANCES (7 habits)

35. Check your bank balance. Just look at the number — no spiraling required.
36. Cancel one subscription you haven't used in the last month.
37. Make a list before your next grocery shop and stick to it.
38. Unsubscribe from 3 promotional emails you never open.
39. Pack or prep your own lunch once this week instead of buying it.
40. Review one bill or recurring charge and confirm it's still worth it.
41. Write down one purchase from this week you didn't need. No judgment — just notice.

---

### RELATIONSHIPS & CONNECTION (9 habits)

42. Send a "thinking of you" message to someone you haven't contacted in a while.
43. Reply to one message you've been putting off. One sentence is enough.
44. Put your phone away completely for one meal today.
45. Make eye contact and actually listen during your next conversation — no half-listening.
46. Say thank you to someone with one specific detail: "thank you for ___."
47. Call someone instead of texting them. Just once today.
48. Remember and acknowledge one upcoming birthday or event for someone you care about.
49. Ask someone how they're really doing — and wait for the real answer.
50. Tell one person one specific thing you appreciate about them.

## BONUS 2 — 7-DAY EMERGENCY RESET

**For when life derails and you need a way back in.**

This mini-reset is for the hard weeks — when work piles up, something goes wrong, or you just fall off completely. It's not a substitute for the full 30 days. It's a bridge back.

Seven days. One small thing each day. That's all.

---

### Emergency Reset — Day 1: Just Show Up

**Task:** Drink a full glass of water. Go to bed at a reasonable time tonight. Nothing else is required.
**Reflection:** What happened? Write one honest sentence about why things derailed.
**Quick Win:** Read this page. You've already started.

---

### Emergency Reset — Day 2: One Familiar Thing

**Task:** Do one habit from your 30-day workbook — any one. The one that felt easiest.
**Reflection:** What does it feel like to do one small thing on a hard day?
**Quick Win:** Set a reminder for the same time tomorrow.

---

### Emergency Reset — Day 3: Clear Something

**Task:** Spend 5 minutes clearing one small physical space — a surface, a bag, a drawer.
**Reflection:** Did the space feel different after? What does that tell you?
**Quick Win:** Wipe the surface down. That's the full task.

---

### Emergency Reset — Day 4: Empty Your Head

**Task:** Set a 5-minute timer and write everything in your head — worries, tasks, feelings. All of it, uncensored.
**Reflection:** Read what you wrote. Is there one thing on that list you can actually do something about today?
**Quick Win:** Cross off one item — either by doing it or by deciding it doesn't need your energy right now.

---

### Emergency Reset — Day 5: Move

**Task:** Walk outside for 10 minutes. No phone, no podcast.
**Reflection:** How did your body feel before vs. after?
**Quick Win:** Stand up and stretch for 60 seconds right now, before the walk.

---

### Emergency Reset — Day 6: Name One Good Thing

**Task:** Write 3 things that are going okay right now. They can be small. They should be honest.
**Reflection:** Why is it hard to notice what's okay when things are hard?
**Quick Win:** Say one of the three things out loud.

---

### Emergency Reset — Day 7: Come Back

**Task:** Read your original Day 1 reflection from the main workbook. Then read your Week 1 checkpoint.
**Reflection:** What do you notice about the gap between who you were on Day 1 and who you are right now?
**Quick Win:** Decide: go back to the main workbook, or run another 7-day emergency reset first. Either is fine.

> You made it through the hard part. Coming back is always the right call.
