## WEEK 1 — FOUNDATION: Energy & Awareness

This week is light on purpose.

You're not here to overhaul your routine or set big goals. You're here to notice — how you feel, what's draining you, and where there might be a little more room to breathe.

Seven small days. Seven small steps. That's all.

---

### Day 1: Check In With Yourself
**Goal:** Notice how you actually feel right now — without trying to fix anything.
**Task 1:** Set a 5-minute timer and sit quietly without your phone. Just notice what comes up — thoughts, body tension, your mood.
**Task 2:** Write 3 words that describe how you've been feeling lately. No editing, just what's true right now.
**Task 3:** Write one word for how you want to feel by the end of these 30 days. Put it somewhere you'll see it tomorrow morning.
**Quick Win:** Take 3 slow breaths before you do anything else today.
**Why It Works:** Naming emotions reduces their intensity — a well-documented effect called affect labeling.
**Reflection:** What's one thing you've been carrying lately that you haven't said out loud to anyone?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow is about your mornings — notice how you wake up and how you feel in the first 10 minutes.

> You made it to Day 1. That's not nothing. Starting is often the hardest part — and you did it.

### Day 2: Your Morning Signal
**Goal:** Notice what your mornings feel like before you change anything about them.
**Task 1:** When you wake up tomorrow, notice the very first thought that comes into your head. Write it down before you do anything else.
**Task 2:** Write down the first thing you typically do each morning and honestly note how it makes you feel.
**Task 3:** Close your eyes and picture tomorrow morning going well. Write one word for how you want that morning to feel.
**Quick Win:** Set your phone across the room tonight so tomorrow's start is a little calmer.
**Why It Works:** Morning routines activate the brain's planning circuits, making the rest of the day feel more manageable.
**Reflection:** What does a good morning feel like to you? What would need to be true for that to happen more often?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow you'll notice one small thing that's been quietly draining your energy.

### Day 3: One Good Thing
**Goal:** Find evidence that things aren't entirely wrong — even if the evidence is small.
**Task 1:** Write down 3 things that went okay today. They don't have to be good — just okay is enough to count.
**Task 2:** Think of one person who makes you feel steadier. Write one sentence about what it is about them that does that.
**Task 3:** Look around the space you're in right now and find one thing you actually like about it.
**Quick Win:** Name one thing that made you smile this week, even briefly. Write it down.
**Why It Works:** Noticing small positives rewires attention patterns over time — a core principle in behavioral science.
**Reflection:** What's one small thing that's been going right lately that you haven't given yourself credit for?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow is about your physical space — one corner, one drawer, one surface.

> You've made it three days. Small wins add up more than you think — and you're already proving that.

### Day 4: Clear One Space
**Goal:** Create one small area that feels calm and a little more like yours.
**Task 1:** Look around your space right now. Write one sentence about how the order or clutter of it makes you feel.
**Task 2:** Put back only what belongs there. Everything else goes in a drawer, a box, or the bin.
**Task 3:** Spend 2 minutes in that cleared space. Sit or stand there. Just notice how it feels different.
**Quick Win:** Wipe the surface down with a cloth. A clean space signals calm to your brain immediately.
**Why It Works:** Visual clutter competes for attention — removing it lowers background stress without any other effort.
**Reflection:** Is there a space in your home that makes you feel tired just looking at it? What's one thing you could remove?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow you'll track your energy at different times of day — no changes yet, just noticing.

### Day 5: Your Energy Map
**Goal:** Notice when your energy rises and falls so you can start working with it instead of against it.
**Task 1:** At three points today — morning, afternoon, evening — pause and rate your energy 1–5. Write the numbers down.
**Task 2:** Look at what you wrote. When do you feel sharpest? When do you fade? Write one sentence about the pattern.
**Task 3:** Write one sentence about when you feel most drained during the day and what usually seems to trigger it.
**Quick Win:** Drink a glass of water right now. Mild dehydration is one of the most common hidden energy drains.
**Why It Works:** Matching tasks to natural energy rhythms increases focus without requiring more willpower.
**Reflection:** What do you usually do during your lowest-energy hour of the day? Is that working for you?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow is about sleep — not fixing it yet, just getting an honest picture of what's actually happening.

### Day 6: Your Sleep Reality
**Goal:** Get an honest picture of your sleep this week without judgment.
**Task 1:** Write down your typical bedtime, wake time, and how rested you feel most mornings on a scale of 1–5.
**Task 2:** Name one thing that regularly cuts into your sleep — screen time, stress, late eating, noise. Just name it.
**Task 3:** Write one sentence about how your current sleep quality affects the way you feel and relate to people during the day.
**Quick Win:** Turn on Do Not Disturb before you go to sleep tonight.
**Why It Works:** Sleep quality affects mood, focus, and appetite regulation more than almost any other single factor.
**Reflection:** What would feel different in your daily life if you were consistently more rested?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow wraps Week 1 — you'll look back at what you noticed and name one thing that already shifted.

### Day 7: Week 1 Wrap
**Goal:** Acknowledge what you've noticed this week and name one small thing that's already different.
**Task 1:** Flip back through Days 1–6. Read what you wrote. Don't analyze — just read and notice what stands out.
**Task 2:** Write one thing you noticed about yourself this week that surprised you or just felt true.
**Task 3:** Write one thing you want to carry into Week 2 — a habit, a thought, or a small action that helped.
**Quick Win:** Put a mark or star on today's date somewhere visible — a sticky note, journal, or calendar. Week 1 is done.
**Why It Works:** Reflection consolidates learning. Reviewing what worked makes the behavior more likely to continue.
**Reflection:** What's one thing you did this week, however small, that you're glad you did?

**Tomorrow:** Week 2 starts tomorrow — it's about momentum. Small habits, gently built, one at a time.
