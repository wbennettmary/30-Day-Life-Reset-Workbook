## WEEK 2 — MOMENTUM: Habits & Productivity

Last week was about noticing. This week is about starting — carefully and without pressure.

You'll build one morning habit this week. One. That's the entire ask. The goal isn't a perfect routine. It's proof that you can choose a small behavior and stick with it for a few days. That proof matters more than the habit itself.

Days 8–10 are a gentle bridge from Week 1. Days 11–14 are where the habit work begins.

---

### Day 8: Coming Back
**Goal:** Return to the workbook without guilt — whether you missed one day or several.
**Task 1:** Read back over your Day 7 wrap. Remind yourself what you noticed. One minute, no pressure.
**Task 2:** Pick one task from any day you missed. Just one. Do that one thing today.
**Task 3:** Write one honest sentence about what the last few days looked like. Just a note, nothing more.
**Quick Win:** Refill your water and sit down for 2 quiet minutes before you do anything else today.
**Why It Works:** Returning after a gap without self-judgment is itself a skill — and one of the most valuable ones to build.
**Reflection:** What made it hard to show up? Not to judge it — just to understand it.
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow you'll look at where your attention actually goes during the day — and whether that's by choice.

> Missing days doesn't reset your progress. You're still here, and that's the only thing that matters right now.

### Day 9: Where Your Attention Goes
**Goal:** Notice what you're spending time and attention on — without changing anything yet.
**Task 1:** Pause and notice how your attention felt today. Did it feel scattered, pulled around, or mostly steady? Write one word.
**Task 2:** Think about your last evening. What did you do for most of it? Write it down honestly.
**Task 3:** Make two lists: what you gave attention to today, and what you wish you'd given more attention to.
**Quick Win:** Put your phone face-down for the next 15 minutes. Just notice what that feels like.
**Why It Works:** Awareness of attention patterns is the first step to shifting them — without data, any change is guesswork.
**Reflection:** If a log showed everything you did this week, would it match what you say matters to you?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow is Day 10. You've made it nearly a third of the way through. That's worth pausing for.

### Day 10: Ten Days In
**Goal:** Pause and acknowledge that ten days of showing up is genuinely significant.
**Task 1:** Go back to your Life Audit from the Setup Pages. Rate the same areas again. Notice any shifts, even tiny ones.
**Task 2:** Write one thing that feels even slightly easier or clearer than it did on Day 1. One thing is enough.
**Task 3:** Write what you want Week 2 to focus on for you. Keep it to one area — morning routine, sleep, or focus.
**Quick Win:** Say out loud or write: "Ten days. I'm doing this." Mean it.
**Why It Works:** Mid-point reflection creates a psychological marker that increases follow-through in the second half of any effort.
**Reflection:** What would you tell someone else who was at Day 10 and quietly doubting themselves?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow you'll choose and start one morning habit. Small, specific, and entirely yours.

> Ten days in. You've built something real — even if it doesn't feel dramatic. Progress rarely does until you look back.

### Day 11: One Morning Habit
**Goal:** Choose one morning habit and start it today — no elaborate routine, just one thing.
**Task 1:** Choose your habit from this list: drink water before coffee · 5 deep breaths after waking · write 3 lines in a notebook.
**Task 2:** Write it down: "My morning habit this week is ___." Put it somewhere you'll see it when you wake up tomorrow.
**Task 3:** Do your habit right now, or set a specific time tomorrow when you will. Commit to the time, not just the idea.
**Quick Win:** Set a phone reminder 10 minutes after your usual wake time. Label it with your habit name.
**Why It Works:** Attaching a new behavior to a fixed morning window builds consistency faster than waiting to feel motivated.
**Reflection:** Why this habit? What do you hope it quietly gives you over time?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow you'll attach your habit to something you already do — making it much harder to forget.

---

### Day 12: Stack It
**Goal:** Lock in your morning habit by pairing it with something you already do automatically.
**Task 1:** Name your most automatic morning behavior — brewing coffee, brushing teeth, opening curtains. Write it down.
**Task 2:** Write your habit stack out loud: "After I [existing behavior], I will [new habit]." This is your cue.
**Task 3:** Do the full stack this morning. Existing behavior → new habit. Back to back. Write one word about how it felt.
**Quick Win:** Write the stack on a sticky note and place it where the cue happens — by the kettle, sink, or bedside.
**Why It Works:** Habit stacking uses existing neural pathways as triggers, cutting the mental effort needed to start a new behavior.
**Reflection:** What's one habit you already have that you never think about? What made it automatic over time?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow you'll check honestly how the habit has been landing — without pressure on the result.

---

### Day 13: Honest Check-In
**Goal:** Assess how your morning habit is going — honestly, without judging whatever the answer is.
**Task 1:** Rate how consistently you did your morning habit this week from 1–5. Write the number and one reason for it.
**Task 2:** Write what got in the way, if anything — time, energy, forgetting, resistance. Just name it clearly.
**Task 3:** Adjust if needed. Simpler version? Different timing? Different habit? Write the updated version right now.
**Quick Win:** Do your morning habit right now, even if it's the afternoon. Timing matters less than keeping it going.
**Why It Works:** Flexible consistency — adapting without quitting — builds habits more reliably than rigid all-or-nothing thinking.
**Reflection:** What's the difference between a habit that's hard and a habit that's simply wrong for you right now?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Tomorrow wraps Week 2 — you'll note what shifted and decide what to carry into Week 3.

### Day 14: Week 2 Wrap
**Goal:** Acknowledge the habit work you did this week and decide what's worth carrying forward.
**Task 1:** Write a short summary of Week 2. What did you try? What stuck? What didn't? Three to five sentences.
**Task 2:** Decide: keep your morning habit into Week 3, adjust it, or replace it with something that fits better.
**Task 3:** Write one thing this week taught you about how you actually work — not about habits, about yourself.
**Quick Win:** Mark Day 14 on your tracker or calendar. Two weeks done.
**Why It Works:** Turning experience into a brief written summary helps the brain store it as a lesson rather than just an event.
**Reflection:** What made this week harder than Week 1? What made it more meaningful?
**Mood:** 😊 / 😐 / 😔
**Tomorrow:** Week 3 starts tomorrow — body, mind, and environment. It's a noticeable shift. You're ready for it.
